About this project
The problem
Line managers didn't know when to escalate — and HR paid the price
In high-volume HR environments, the highest driver of wasted HR time is not case complexity — it is poor initial triage. Managers escalate informally-resolvable issues to formal processes too early, or sit on serious matters too long. Without a consistent first-response framework, every case lands in HR's inbox the same way: urgent, undocumented, and missing half the context needed to act.

What I built
A manager-facing triage tool — not an HR reference document
Most ER frameworks are built for HR professionals and live as static Word documents that managers never read. This tool was designed from the opposite direction: starting with the questions a line manager actually asks when a people issue lands on their desk, and routing them — without HR jargon — to the right first response. The language is deliberate. "Has a formal process already been started?" is a question a manager can answer. "Identify the procedural stage" is not.
Design decisions
Why it works the way it does
⚖
Complexity scoring
Every triage path now produces a complexity score (Low / Medium / High) with an estimated HR time cost. This exists because not all cases consume equal resource — and HR teams need to triage their own time, not just the case.
🔒
COI check first
Conflict of interest is checked before any triage begins. A missed COI that surfaces mid-investigation is one of the most common causes of procedural unfairness — and one of the easiest to prevent.
📋
ET pathway included
Most triage frameworks stop at internal appeal. This one continues to ACAS Early Conciliation and ET referral — because that's the actual end of the process, and HR needs to know what happens to documentation after a claim is filed.
🗓
Sickness sub-typed
Short-term, pattern, long-term (welfare stage), and long-term approaching capability all route differently. Treating them the same is one of the most common manager errors — and one of the most common sources of disability discrimination risk.
What's not in scope
Limitations I'd build next
This version does not include a safeguarding pathway (critical in education, healthcare, and any organisation working with vulnerable people), union-specific steps for unionised environments, or GDPR handling guidance within the intake form. A future version would also integrate with a live case management system rather than simulating routing. The framework is also UK-law specific — a multi-jurisdiction version would require significant additional branching.
